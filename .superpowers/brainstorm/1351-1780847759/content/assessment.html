AE Pages: What's Working vs. What Could Be Better

My read after going through the hub and both detail pages

✓ What's already strong

  • Detail pages are genuinely impressive — Engineering Snapshot, stat boxes, phase-numbered sections, Key Takeaways grid, and captioned figures all read like a real engineering portfolio, not a class assignment printout.
  • Writing quality is high — technically accurate, first-person, professional tone. The Turbofan page especially reads like something an employer would actually read.
  • Animations add polish — the fadeInUp on stat boxes and hover lift on takeaway cards are subtle and work well.

↑ Where there's room to improve

1. Hub page feels plain vs. the detail pages

Eight identical-looking cards with no visual hierarchy. A recruiter can't tell the senior design project apart from a one-off controls lab at a glance. No tags, no role indicators, no way to scan.

2. No project tags or context badges

Visitors can't see at a glance: Is this a group project? What tools were used? What discipline — propulsion, controls, structures, orbital? Small tags on each hub card would tell that story instantly.

3. Detail pages vary in depth (inconsistency)

The Turbofan and DC-8 pages are rich and well-structured. Other detail pages may not be built to the same level — inconsistent quality undermines the overall impression.

4. HTML structure issue on hub page

Each project card uses <ul><p>...</p></ul> — a <p> inside a <ul> is invalid HTML. Should be a plain <p> with no <ul> wrapper.

5. No breadcrumb / "you are here" on detail pages

There's a "← Back to Aerospace Projects" link at the bottom only. Adding a simple breadcrumb at the top (Aerospace → Turbofan) makes navigation feel cleaner and shows hierarchy at a glance.

6. Copyright says 2030

Footer on all pages shows © 2030. Probably a small oversight — should be 2025 (or left open-ended as © 2025–present).
